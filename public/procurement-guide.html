Add Your Suppliers

Sidebar → Suppliers

➊

Open Suppliers

Click Suppliers in the left sidebar.

➋

Click "Add Supplier"

Top-right corner of the page.

➌

Fill in the details

At minimum, enter the Company Name. Everything else is optional but recommended.

➍

Save

Click Add Supplier. Repeat for all your vendors.

Fields Explained

Company Name
Required
The vendor's trading name, e.g. "Fresh Foods Trading"
Contact Person
Optional
The sales rep you deal with
Phone / WhatsApp
Optional
Clickable link on the card — opens dialer
Email
Optional
Clickable link — opens mail client
Payment Terms
Required
Cash on Delivery, Net 15, Net 30, Net 45, Net 60, or Prepaid
Notes
Optional
Delivery days, special conditions, anything you need to remember
⚠️
Do this first

You must have at least one supplier before you can create Purchase Orders or RFQs. Add all your vendors here before proceeding.

Set Up Inventory Items

Sidebar → Inventory

➊

Open Inventory

Click Inventory in the left sidebar.

➋

Click "Add Item"

Top-right corner. Fill in the form for each ingredient or supply.

➌

Set your Alert Below threshold

This is the minimum stock level before a warning appears. Set it to about 2–3 days of usage.

➍

Save the item

Click Add Item. Repeat for everything you want to track.

Fields Explained

Name
Required
e.g. "Chicken Breast", "Olive Oil", "Takeaway Cups"
Category
Required
Produce, Meat, Seafood, Dairy, Beverages, Dry Goods, etc.
Unit
Required
How you measure it — kg, L, pcs, box, bag, bottle, etc.
Current Stock
Required
How much you have right now
Alert Below
Required
Minimum level before a low-stock warning is triggered
Cost per Unit
Optional
Default purchase price — used to calculate total inventory value
✅
Why linking matters

When a Purchase Order is received, stock auto-updates — but only for items linked to inventory. Always pick from the dropdown when building a PO.

Create a Purchase Order Directly

Purchases → Purchase Orders tab

➊

Go to Purchases → Purchase Orders

Click Purchases in the sidebar, then the Purchase Orders tab.

➋

Click "Create PO"

Top-right of the tab.

➌

Select a Supplier & set the date

Pick from the dropdown. Set an expected delivery date if you know it.

➍

Add line items

Pick from the Inventory Item dropdown — it auto-fills name, unit, and cost. Enter quantity. Use "Custom" for one-off items not in inventory.

➎

Click "Create Draft PO"

The PO is saved. Review it before marking as Sent.

Purchase Order Lifecycle

Draft

Created, not yet sent. Can still edit or cancel.

→
Sent

Click Send after contacting your supplier.

→
Received

Click Receive on delivery. Stock updates automatically.

ℹ️
Cancelling a PO

Only possible in Draft status. Once sent or received, it cannot be cancelled from the system.

Use RFQ to Compare Suppliers

Purchases → RFQs tab

📨 Create the RFQ

  • Go to Purchases → RFQs tab
  • Click New RFQ
  • Set a Required By date
  • Add the items you need with quantities
  • Tick the checkboxes for each supplier to approach
  • Click Create RFQ

✉️ Send & Wait

  • The RFQ is created as Draft
  • Contact suppliers by WhatsApp or email as usual
  • Click Send to mark it as sent in the system
  • Wait for suppliers to reply with prices

📝 Enter Quotes

  • Click the RFQ number to expand it
  • Find the supplier card at the bottom
  • Click Enter Quote
  • Type in their unit price per item — totals auto-calculate
  • Add notes if any, then Save Quote
  • Repeat for each supplier that responds

⭐ Compare & Convert

  • Expand the RFQ to see the comparison table
  • All supplier prices shown side by side
  • Lowest total highlighted in green with a ★
  • On the best supplier's card, click → PO
  • A Create PO modal opens pre-filled — confirm to create
💡
RFQ vs. Direct PO

Use RFQ when sourcing new items or prices are variable. Use a Direct PO for regular trusted suppliers with fixed agreed prices.

Use Templates for Recurring Orders

Purchases → Templates tab

➊

Go to Purchases → Templates

Click the Templates tab inside Purchases.

➋

Click "New Template"

Give it a clear name like "Weekly Produce Order" or "Monthly Cleaning Supplies".

➌

Set a Default Supplier (optional)

If you always order this from the same vendor, select them — it will pre-fill when using the template.

➍

Add items with default quantities & costs

Link to inventory items where possible. Set the typical order quantity and current cost per unit.

➎

Save & Use it

Click Save Template. Next time you need to reorder, click Use Template — a PO modal opens pre-filled. Adjust quantities if needed and create.

🚀
Recommended Templates to Set Up

Weekly produce · Weekly meat & poultry · Bi-weekly dairy · Monthly dry goods · Monthly packaging. Once done, restocking is a 10-second task every week.

Receiving a Delivery

Purchases → Purchase Orders — Do this every time goods arrive

➊

Go to Purchase Orders

Find the PO for this delivery. It should be in Draft or Sent status.

➋

Click "Receive"

The green Receive button appears on any Draft or Sent PO.

➌

Review the confirmation screen

Items shown in green are linked to inventory and will auto-update. Items in grey are custom and won't affect any stock count.

➍

Click "Confirm Received"

Stock updates instantly. PO status changes to Received. Spend is recorded in Reports.

✅
Green items (Linked)

Stock count increases automatically. Check Inventory to see the new balance.

ℹ️
Grey items (Custom)

One-off items not in inventory. No stock count changes — record manually if needed.

⚠️
Important

Only confirm when goods have physically arrived and been checked. This cannot be undone. For short deliveries or damaged goods, adjust stock manually in Inventory afterwards.

Monitor Inventory & Low Stock Alerts

Sidebar → Inventory — Check this daily

🔔

Check the Alert Banner

When any item falls below its threshold, a red/amber banner appears at the top of Inventory showing how many items need attention. Check this every morning.

🔍

Use Filter Tabs to narrow down

Click Low Stock or Out of Stock tabs to see only the items that need ordering. Also filter by category.

➕

Adjust Stock Manually if needed

Click the +/- icon on any item to add or remove stock for waste, spillage, spoilage, or manual counts.

Stock Status Colours

OK

Above the alert threshold

Low

Below threshold — order soon

Out

Zero stock — order immediately

💡
Setting Good Alert Thresholds

Alert Below = 2–3 days of normal usage. If you use 5 kg of chicken daily, set the alert to 15 kg — enough time to place and receive an order before running out.

View in Reports

Sidebar → Reports — See the full financial picture

📊 Key Numbers to Watch

  • Gross Revenue — total sales before discounts
  • Net Revenue — after discounts and VAT
  • Total Expenses — all logged costs
  • Net Profit = Net Revenue minus Total Expenses

📦 Inventory Snapshot

  • Scroll to the bottom of Reports
  • Shows current stock status at a glance
  • Red/amber items have a Create PO shortcut link
  • Good for a quick morning check

How to Log a Manual Expense

➊

Scroll to the Expenses section in Reports

Towards the bottom of the Reports page.

➋

Click "+ Add Expense"

Use for costs not from a PO — rent, utilities, salaries, marketing, etc.

➌

Fill in Category, Date, Amount, Description

Categories: Food & Beverage, Utilities, Rent, Labour, Marketing, Maintenance, Other.

➍

Click Save

The expense reduces your Net Profit immediately.

Tips for Daily Operations

🌅 Morning Routine (2 min)

Open Inventory → check the alert banner. If any items are low or out, go to Purchases and create a PO or use a template.

🔗 Always Link to Inventory

When creating a PO, always select items from the Inventory dropdown. This is what makes stock auto-update on receive.

📋 Templates = Time Saved

Set up your top 5 recurring orders as templates. Reordering takes 10 seconds instead of 5 minutes.

💰 Log All Expenses

For accurate profit figures, log non-PO costs in Reports → Expenses: rent, utilities, staff meals, marketing.

⭐ RFQ Every Quarter

Even for regular suppliers, run an RFQ quarterly to make sure you're still getting competitive prices.

🔢 Realistic Thresholds

Alert Below = 2–3 days of usage. Too low and you'll run out. Too high and you'll constantly over-order.
